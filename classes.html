Classes & Schedule

Around a dozen halls, seven days a week

Train at whichever hall and time suits you — every member can attend any class at their grade and age. First lesson is always free.

Class types

Something for every age and grade

Ages 4–14

Junior Karate

Beginners through advanced juniors, building focus, fitness and self-defence basics in a structured, traditional syllabus.

Ages 11–Adult

Senior & Adult Karate

All grades train together through to brown and black belt, with dedicated advanced sessions at most halls.

All Ages

Family Classes

Train alongside your children at our Bromley, Bellingham and Mottingham family sessions — all grades welcome.

Brown & Black Belts

Black Belt Kata

Dedicated kata refinement for brown and black belts, run as extra time at the end of regular hall sessions.

Teens & Adults

Knife Self-Defence

Every 1st & 3rd lesson of the month at Lochaber Hall (Lee/Lewisham). Realistic training with rubber knives — sweeps, takedowns, locks, holds, pressure points and breaks — for self-defence only.

Inclusive Teaching

Additional Needs Welcome

Sensei Tom Tennick has over 30 years' experience teaching students with dyslexia, autism, ADHD, sensory impairments and hearing loss/deafness, most training within the main class. Sensei Sharon GoodwinDoash holds an NVQ Level 3 in British Sign Language.

From Home

Zoom Classes

Optional online sessions for members who want to keep training from home, in addition to hall classes.

Questions before your first class?

Is the first lesson really free?

Yes — your first lesson at any TKI hall is free, with dojo fees only starting from your second lesson.

What should I wear to my first class?

A clean, well-ironed white karate gi is preferred once you join; until then, tracksuit bottoms and a plain white round-neck t-shirt are fine. No jewellery, and nails should be kept short.

Do I need to book a class in advance?

No booking system is needed — just turn up to any class at your grade and age, at any of our halls.

My child has additional needs — can they train?

Yes. Sensei Tom Tennick has over 30 years' experience teaching students with dyslexia, autism, ADHD, sensory impairments and hearing loss or deafness, and Sensei Sharon GoodwinDoash holds an NVQ Level 3 in British Sign Language. Get in touch beforehand and we'll talk through what would help.
